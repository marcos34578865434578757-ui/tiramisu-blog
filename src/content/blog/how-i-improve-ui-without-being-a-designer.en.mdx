## I used to treat “good design” like something mysterious

When a page looked bad, all I could say was: “something feels off.”  
That kind of reaction never helped me improve the page.

So I started forcing myself to be more specific:

- is the hierarchy too flat
- are the title and body competing with each other
- do spacing, cards, and buttons have no shared rhythm

## Now I review pages through four angles

### 1. Hierarchy

Is the main point obvious on the first screen?  
If the title, labels, and supporting copy all compete for attention, the page becomes noisy very quickly.

### 2. Spacing

Whitespace is not emptiness. It gives the content room to breathe.  
When everything is squeezed together, even strong content feels tiring.

### 3. Rhythm

Cards, paragraphs, and buttons all need a consistent rhythm.  
When spacing jumps around randomly, the page starts to feel unstable.

### 4. Readability

Font size, line height, text width, and contrast matter more than decorative effects.  
Whether a page can be read comfortably often decides whether it feels polished.

## AI helps me accelerate experiments, not replace taste

I do not ask AI to decide what beauty means.  
I use it to try different structures, spacing systems, and wording hierarchies faster.

That gives me more chances to compare options and train my own judgment.
