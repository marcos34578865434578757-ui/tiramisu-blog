## What made me nervous was not the deploy command itself

The scary part was not clicking the button.  
It was the question behind it: what if the production version behaves differently from local development?

Locally, everything feels predictable:

- the data is mine
- the routes are already in my browser history
- most assets have already been cached once

But the moment I prepared to ship, the real questions became:

- will the build fail
- did I miss an environment variable
- will the first screen throw a strange runtime error

## I ended up building a repeatable launch checklist

Before I deploy now, I run through three layers of checks.

### 1. Run lint and build first

This is the most obvious step, but also the easiest one to skip.  
If local `lint` and `build` do not pass, production is unlikely to save me.

### 2. Check routes and assets

I make sure:

- cover images and body images load correctly
- post detail pages and project detail pages both open cleanly
- dark mode, language switching, and other interactions still behave as expected

### 3. Inspect the live site after deployment

A successful deployment is not the same as a working experience.  
I still click through the home page, list pages, and detail pages and look for:

- console errors
- broken images
- layout shifts
- interactions that only fail in production

## The biggest mindset change for me

I used to treat “shipping” like a dramatic, advanced milestone.  
After doing it once, I realized it works better as a reusable process.

When the process is clear, deployment stops feeling like pressure and starts becoming part of the normal rhythm of the project.
