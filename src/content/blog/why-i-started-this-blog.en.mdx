## I did not want my work to stay scattered across platforms

My ideas, project fragments, and process notes used to live in too many places.  
Each platform held one piece, but none of them really represented what I was building.

That is why I decided to build this blog seriously and treat it like a space that actually belongs to me.

## This blog is not only for showing finished work

Of course I want it to feel polished over time.  
But more importantly, it needs to hold three kinds of things:

- writing
- projects
- the decisions and retrospectives that follow each iteration

So it is not just a static portfolio page. It is an active workspace that keeps growing.

## I also want to preserve the path, not only the result

Often the most valuable part is not the finished page.  
It is seeing how the page was built, corrected, and shipped step by step.

I want to be able to look back later and still understand:

- where I got stuck
- how AI helped me move forward
- which decisions turned out to matter

That is why I want to keep documenting the process instead of only showing the outcome.
