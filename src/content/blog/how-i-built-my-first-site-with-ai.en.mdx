## What I lacked at the beginning was not code, but structure

At first, syntax was not even the main blocker.  
The bigger problem was that I genuinely did not know how to break a website down.

That is where AI became useful for me.  
Not because it could generate an entire site in one shot, but because it helped me split the problem into smaller questions:

- what should the first screen communicate
- which pages need to exist first
- how should components be organized so the site does not feel messy

## I slowly learned to treat AI like a working partner

At the beginning, I wanted AI to hand me a complete answer.  
Later I found a better loop:

1. explain what I want to build
2. ask AI to break the page structure down
3. refine one module at a time
4. come back with my own judgment and clean the weak spots

This feels less magical than instant generation, but it produces better decisions and leaves behind a reusable process.

## The real skill I want to keep training is judgment

Once the page actually ran, I realized the real work had only started.

- is the copy clear enough
- does the visual hierarchy have a real focus
- is this page worth returning to

AI lowered the cost of getting started.  
But what makes a site feel like finished work is still the repeated cycle of reviewing and refining it.
